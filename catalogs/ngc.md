# NGC Catalog

The **New General Catalogue (NGC)** is a catalog of deep-sky astronomical objects such as star clusters, nebulae, and galaxies. It was compiled in the 19th century by John Louis Emil Dreyer.

## What it contains

The NGC includes thousands of objects, including:

- Galaxies
- Open clusters
- Globular clusters
- Nebulae
- Planetary nebulae

## Example NGC Objects

| Object | Type | Constellation | Notes |
|--------|------|---------------|-------|
| NGC 224 | Galaxy | Andromeda | Also known as the Andromeda Galaxy (M31) |
| NGC 6205 | Globular Cluster | Hercules | Also known as Messier 13 |
| NGC 1952 | Supernova Remnant | Taurus | Also known as the Crab Nebula (M1) |
| NGC 1976 | Nebula | Orion | Also known as the Orion Nebula (M42) |

## Why the NGC matters

The NGC is one of the most widely used deep-sky catalogs in astronomy and is still commonly referenced by amateur and professional astronomers.
